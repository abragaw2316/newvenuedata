# Phase 5 — Weighted Scoring Model

> **Goal:** Convert Phase 2 (opportunity) + Phase 3 (competitive winnability) into one transparent, weighted score per finalist, aligned to the founder's stated optimization priorities.
> **Date:** 2026-06-14 · **Scope:** the 10 niches that survived Phase 3 (the rest were filtered out in Phase 2). Scores 1–5 (5 = best). All weights and math shown.

---

## Weighting framework (derived from the founder's 5 explicit priorities)

The founder said optimize STRICTLY for: ① highest probability of success · ② highest probability of recurring revenue · ③ fastest path to paying customers · ④ long-term scalability · ⑤ sustainable competitive advantage — and explicitly NOT for raw TAM / VC-scale. The weights below encode exactly that (TAM is deliberately only 5%):

| Criterion | Weight | Maps to priority |
|---|---|---|
| **Defensibility / moat** | 15% | ⑤ sustainable advantage |
| **Speed to first customer** | 13% | ③ fastest path |
| **Solo-AI winnability** (Phase 3) | 12% | ① probability of success |
| **Recurring / subscription fit** | 12% | ② recurring revenue |
| **Scalability / expansion** | 11% | ④ scalability |
| **Low competition** | 10% | ⑤ sustainable advantage |
| **Customer willingness-to-pay** | 10% | ② + ① |
| **Speed to MVP** | 7% | ③ fastest path |
| **AI-automation edge** | 5% | (margin + founder's edge) |
| **Revenue potential / TAM** | 5% | (capped — not optimizing for scale) |
| **Total** | **100%** | |

*Sustainable advantage (defensibility + low competition) = 25%. Probability of success (winnability + speed) = ~32%. Recurring (rec + WTP) = 22%. These are intentionally the heaviest, per the brief.*

## What the model says (and its honest limits)

1. **The top 4 are a statistical dead heat (4.02 → 3.93, spread 0.09).** Liquor triggers, STR, new-business formation, and mechanics liens are all excellent; the model's #1 is a *nose*, not a blowout. Differentiation comes down to the **speed-vs-ceiling trade-off** and founder fit.
2. **Liquor triggers win on speed + lowest competition** (MVP 5, low-competition 5) — the fastest path to a first paying customer, against essentially no real competitor.
3. **Mechanics liens and STR win on defensibility (4)** — bigger moats, slightly slower starts. Higher single-vertical ceilings.
4. **The fast/national niches confirm Phase 3:** FMCSA (3.83), solar (3.76), recompetes & grid (3.65) all get dragged down by **defensibility 2–3 + low-competition 2–3** despite elite speed/WTP scores. The weighting (which the founder's own priorities dictated) correctly penalizes "easy data anyone can copy."
5. **TAM barely matters here by design** — at 5% weight, raw market size can't rescue a low-defensibility niche, exactly as the founder requested ("do not optimize for VC scale").
